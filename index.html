    Fretboard & chords
    
Every string starts open. Tap a fret to move that string’s note, or tap its open note on the nut to mute it. When a chord is recognised, use the small ◀ ▶ arrows beside the Chord tab to browse shapes—including barre-family grips—then use Add to… to place it in the active song section. Suggestions show matching ◀ / ▶ controls only when there are more chords to explore; you can also drag the row sideways or use its arrow keys.

  

  

    Solo / arpeggio
    
Switch modes and a deliberately selected chord becomes the active Solo selection, ready to save as a chord hit or reshape into an arpeggio. Saving it or pressing Clear selection removes that one-shot reference; choose or edit a chord again to start another. For Slide, Hammer-on, or Pull-off: select the source, press the technique, choose its destination, then press Save. Slides infer up or down from the fret change. In both the draft and Song, a dashed card marked “starts … →” is the source and the red-bordered card marked “from N” is its destination. Dragging either end moves the whole connected phrase together. Use Notes / Tab to change the display, use the ×N menu to repeat a unit, and ⧉ to duplicate an editable event or whole linked phrase.

  

  

    Saving & editing a solo
    
Create solo adds a new draft to an active chord section or creates a Solo section. Edit solo loads it back into the recorder; the button becomes Save solo. Click any numbered event to load its notes onto the fretboard, adjust them, then choose a technique to update that event in place.

  

  

    Song sections
    
Click a section to make it active. Drag chord chips to reorder or move them; drag the ⋮⋮ handle to reorder whole sections. Use ⇢ to copy or move every chord, Solo phrase, and lyric into another section. Use × to delete.

  

  

    Key, scales & notes
    
Key & Scales suggests a musical home for your song. One chord is marked Tentative; more chords give stronger direction. When a scale is on the fretboard, its name appears in the collapsed header. Click the active scale card again to turn it off. Use ♯ / ♭ to change note spelling.

  

  

    Chord suggestions
    
In Chord mode, the strip below the fretboard offers core diatonic chords first, then richer in-scale colours. Use its ◀ / ▶ controls, arrow keys, or a swipe to move through full groups of suggestions. Choosing a full scale such as Harmonic minor changes the chord pool; pentatonic and blues scales re-centre the order without pretending to define every harmony.

  

  

    Offline, sharing & privacy
    
Your work auto-saves on this device. Back up a project for safekeeping, preview/export tab to share it. After the first visit, the app works offline. No account or tracking is used.
